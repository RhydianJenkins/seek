Understanding Vector Embeddings for RAG

Published on January 15, 2024 by Jane Smith

What are Vector Embeddings?

Vector embeddings are numerical representations of text that capture semantic meaning. Unlike traditional keyword search, embeddings allow us to find semantically similar content even when the exact words don't match.

How RAG Uses Embeddings

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems use embeddings in three key steps:

  • Chunk documents into smaller pieces
  • Convert each chunk into a vector embedding
  • Store embeddings in a vector database
  • Retrieve relevant chunks based on semantic similarity

Best Practices

When implementing RAG systems, consider these important factors:

  1. Chunk size: Balance between context and precision (typically 256-512 tokens)
  2. Overlap: Use sliding windows to prevent context loss at boundaries
  3. Metadata: Include source information for better traceability
The key to effective RAG is ensuring your embeddings capture the right level of semantic granularity for your use case.

Conclusion

Vector embeddings are the foundation of modern RAG systems, enabling semantic search that goes beyond simple keyword matching.
